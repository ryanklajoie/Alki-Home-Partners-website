Why This Exists

The home has always needed
an operator. Now it has one.

"She had done well. But the house had deteriorated around her — not because she lacked resources, but because nobody was there to hold the thread."

In February 2026, my aunt passed away at 78 in the house where she had spent her adult life — a house my grandfather had built himself. She had worked concurrent careers as a nurse and a teacher. She owned her home outright. She had done well financially.

But the house had deteriorated around her. Not because she lacked resources — but because nobody was there to hold the thread. No trusted partner. No one to assess what the house needed, plan for it, and make sure it got done.

She would have had a more dignified end of life if that relationship had existed. That is not an edge case. It is a failure of the market. It is the reason Alki Home Partners exists.

The owner-occupied home is the largest financial commitment most people make, and it is almost entirely self-managed by people with no training to do it. The contractor market is fragmented. Inspectors document condition once and leave. Nobody has eyes on the asset between transactions. The result is a population of well-intentioned homeowners making reactive, uninformed decisions about a $700K–$2M+ asset.

We're here to change that.

Business Acumen

The Right Combination

This business requires someone who can credibly operate at the intersection of trades, property, and planning. Enough trade capability to do the work and evaluate other contractors' work. Enough sales experience to build a referral channel. Enough business acumen to structure a defensible long-term model. Most people can do one of these. This business requires all three.

Aging in Place Focus

Pursuing CAPS certification (Certified Aging in Place Specialist, NAHB). The AIP channel — elder law attorneys, geriatric care managers, senior real estate specialists — is a structured, directly saleable referral network with no current trusted operator to hand home management to. That asymmetry is a real strategic advantage.
